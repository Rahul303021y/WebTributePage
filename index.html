Dilip Joshi aka "Jethalal"

Dilip Joshi (born 26 May 1965) is an Indian film, television actor who is best known for portraying main role in the long-running Indian (Hindi) television sitcom Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah.

BCA

Personal Life

While doing BCA, he was awarded the INT (Indian National Theater) Best Actor

Career

Dilip Joshi started his acting career in 1989, playing the character of Ramu in the film Maine Pyar Kiya. Later, he appeared in several Gujarati dramas, one of them being Bapu Tame Kamaal Kari with Sumeet Raghavan and Amit Mistry, the trio known for their television show Shubh Mangal Savadhan. Joshi starred in the show Yeh Duniya Hai Rangeen and Kya Baat Hai in which he played a South Indian. He also appeared in the films Phir Bhi Dil Hai Hindustani and Hum Aapke Hain Koun..! Joshi with his co-star Disha Vakani in success bash for completion of 3836 episodes of TMKOC. During 2008, Joshi began a long run playing the role of Jethalal Champaklal Gada in the popular sitcom Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah. For his performance in the show, he received several accolades, including 5 Telly awards and 3 ITA awards. Notable among his other tele-serials in Hindi are Kabhi Yeh Kabhi Woh, Hum Sab Baarati, Hum Sab Ek Hain, Shubh Mangal Savadhan, Kya Baat Hai, Daal Mein Kala and Meri Biwi Wonderful. He appeared in the children's comedy Agadam Bagdam Tigdam as Uncle Tappu, as well as in the 2009 films Dhoondte Reh Jaaoge and Ashutosh Gowarikar's What's Your Raashee.

The store sees tons of drama involving Jetha, Nattu Kaka and Bagha.Jethalal Champaklal Gada is a beloved character from the Indian television sitcom "Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah." Portrayed by the talented actor Dilip Joshi, Jethalal is an endearing and comic figure that has captured the hearts of audiences across generations. He is the owner of "Gada Electronics," a small electronics shop in the fictional Gokuldham Society. Jethalal is known for his childlike enthusiasm, innocent charm, and knack for landing himself in hilarious and often absurd situations. His interactions with his family, neighbors, and friends, especially his misadventures with his father Champaklal and his best friend Taarak Mehta, are the highlights of the show. One of the central elements of Jethalal's character is his unwavering devotion to his wife, Daya Jethalal Gada, who lovingly addresses him as "Tapu ke papa." His affectionate relationship with his son, Tipendra Gada (Tapu), showcases his caring and doting side as a father. Jethalal's innocence often leads him into humorous and sticky predicaments, and his hilarious attempts to extricate himself from such situations have left the audience in splits. Dilip Joshi's impeccable comic timing and portrayal of Jethalal's character have made him an iconic figure in Indian television history. Overall, Jethalal is a character that exudes warmth, humor, and relatability, making him an integral part of the success of "Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah" and endearing him to millions of viewers.
